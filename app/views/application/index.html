Win-Win

Paula paid £237.85 to fly from Bordeaux to Tenerife with Spanish airline Volotea. Now she has to cancel the trip. If Paula decides to fly at some later date, she will have to pay £85.97 in airline fees (36% additional cost). Paula is likely to let the ticket expire (now or later), losing £237.85.

But she now posts the ticket on Theia and finds Alex, who is willing to pay £239.86 for Paula’s ticket (airfare has gone up to £266.51 since Paula booked it). Paula sells the ticket and recovers 67% of the original ticket and fees, after transaction costs... and Alex bought it at a 10% discount to the current airline price.

Alex is now the passenger on record and travels like any other passenger. Paula has been refunded so she can spend the money as she wishes, without restrictions. Win-Win

Crystal clear

How much would I recover when my ticket is sold?

The recovery value will depend upon the destination, date, time to departure and demand for your ticket.
We will give you an estimated recovery rate, normally higher than 60%, and will try and negotiate the best possible deal so both you and your fellow surrogate traveller achieve the best possible outcome.

Would I get more or less than what I paid for the ticket in the first place?

Under normal circumstances you will need to sell at a discount to the price originally paid. We will reimburse you 100% of fees applied by your trusted airline and less than 100% of the value of the ticket. Overall, you will be recovering money from a ticket you will no longer use.

Is it possible to transfer my ticket to someone else?

Some airlines allow transferability of non-refundable tickets, as long as fees and tariff differentials are paid for. Additionally, there are some procedures that the airlines require before transferring a ticket.
We will find a buyer and make this process as smooth as possible so you can get your cash and crack on.

What if I didn’t buy the ticket but someone else bought it for me to travel?

We can still execute the transfer, however we will need to get in touch with the person who paid for the ticket. All refunds and proceeds will go to the person who bought the ticket and not the person named to travel on the ticket (if they are different).

Is this for domestic or international flights?

Theia works on any flight and destination, as long as your trusted airline has a name change policy in place.

Which airlines have a name change policy in place?

In Europe, Ryanair, easyJet, Air Berlin, Volotea, Vueling, Norwegian Air, Jet2, Eurowings and others. In the US, Southwest, Frontier and Hawaiian Airlines.
This list is expanding as we speak, if your airline is not here don’t assume they wouldn’t work with us, send us an email mentioning your airline and we’ll get an answer for you.

Does this work for round trip as well as one-way flights?

Theia will match passengers at any stage of the itinerary, even if you have already started the trip and want to stay where you are (like in a movie). As soon as we get clearance from the participating airline, we will take care of the rest.

If I’m selling my unused ticket, why do I have to pay additional charges?

This is a requirement from your trusted airline, not from us. Your airline requires you to pay any fees applicable to transferring the ticket, and you pay it directly to the airline, not to us.
We will recover this amount and give you a full refund on any applicable fees, plus a recovery rate on the value of the ticket you’re selling.

How does this not trigger security concerns?

Theia complies with security protocols because all passenger information involved is retained and controlled by the participating airline.
If a passenger has been blacklisted and is not able to book a ticket with the airline in the first place, they will not be able to get a ticket in the platform.

How do I pay for the ticket if I’m buying on Theia?

Initially you can pay via PayPal (you are covered by PayPal’s Purchase Protection. Please see PayPal’s User Agreement for details on rights and coverage).
We are in the process of adding major credit cards as accepted payment methods.

If I buy a ticket on the platform, how do I know I will be able to actually fly?

Once we have completed the transfer procedure, your name will be immediately updated on the airline's booking system as the travelling passenger. You will be able to immediately confirm directly on the airline’s website that you are the travelling passenger.

If I buy a ticket on the platform from another passenger and then decide not to fly, can I sell the ticket?

Of course, it is your ticket and you can do whatever you want with it. Come back to us and we will locate a willing passenger for your ticket and execute the transfer again.

Would I get a refund if I buy a ticket on the platform and then I cannot use it?

Tickets sold on the platform are normally non-refundable tickets, as specified by participating airlines. We suggest you put the ticket back on the platform and we will find a willing traveller that can buy it from you.

Can I buy allocated seats, extra luggage, priority boarding and other services on a ticket bought on Theia?

Absolutely. Once the ticket has been transferred to your name, you are a passenger like any other, free to use any ancillary services you may want from participating airlines.
Additionally, if the original passenger included any of these services in the ticket, they will be transferred to you as the legal holder of the ticket and travelling passenger.
